Bottom Line

Tesla is a fundamentally improving business masquerading as an astronomically priced AI option. Q1 2026 showed genuine margin recovery — gross margin at 21.1% is the strongest in two years, EPS beat estimates by 11%, and free cash flow is solidly positive. But deliveries missed, inventory is building, energy revenue fell 12%, and the company is spending $25B+ in capex in 2026 alone. The valuation at 387× earnings embeds Robotaxi, Optimus, and FSD licence revenue that is not yet material. Consensus is a Hold (avg. price target ~$400–$406), with bulls targeting $550–$600 on autonomous upside and bears pointing to a $200–$250 de-rating scenario if Cybercab timelines slip. Risk score of 65/100 reflects a strong balance sheet and real margin momentum partially overwhelmed by one of the most stretched valuations in large-cap equity markets.
